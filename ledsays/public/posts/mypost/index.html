Web 1 & 2

October 11, 2022

Hi, I’m David a.k.a LED

Welcome to my blog, where I will post a loose collection of thoughts (or ramblings) about my foray into what some see as the next generation of the web, all the way back from my beginnings in computing.

HTML websites were abundant and each still had their own quirky colours, sounds, clip art, gifs and hyperlinks.. for days. Navigation was messy and pressing on the wrong link which ended up loading a 3 megabyte picture was utterly painful on such a slow connection. More often then not, by the time you realised it was the wrong link, it was too late.

Being a millenial I was blessed (or cursed) to have lived through the days of listening to the dial tone of my 56k modem everytime I even thought about accessing the internet.

Once connected I would hit up my high schools’ unofficial mIRC chatroom and open MSN Messenger. Next I’d open Winamp and blast whatever CD I had purchased/pirated most recently. For this example of a purchase I’ll go with LIVE’s 1994 album, Throwing Copper.

Finally, I would boot up ‘Diablo II: Lord of Destruction’ and confirm it was working by hearing my CD-ROM drive go from an idle hum to spinning furiously. My CRT monitor dimmed to black and each letter of the Blizzard logo ignited sequentially. After the illustriously blinding white Blizzard North logo literally blew passed, the games title screen would dimly light the room and the best game soundtrack I’d ever heard would commence.

This game had a profound effect on me because of the large community on Battle.net. I was lucky enough to live in a court with two other families with kids my age who both managed to get the game. Over the years we would theorycraft different character builds, experimenting with different gear and skill synergy allocations, compete in PvP games and attempt to trade our way up to the best gear. Rinse and repeat.

To me, a community is a place where people can communicate their experiences, seek and provide knowledge or advice, and belong with the freedom to come and go.
